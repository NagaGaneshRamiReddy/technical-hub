Publishing Flutter App for iOS

Introduction

Publishing a Flutter app on iOS requires setting up Xcode, configuring your app for release, creating an Apple Developer account, and submitting your app to the App Store. This guide walks through the necessary steps to successfully release your Flutter app for iOS.

Step 1: Update the App Version and Build Number

Before building your app for release, ensure that your appâ€™s version and build number are correctly configured in pubspec.yaml. The version and build number should follow the format version: x.y.z+build:

version: 1.0.0+1

In iOS, this version information is reflected in Xcode. Open the ios/Runner.xcworkspace file in Xcode and ensure that the version and build number are correct in the General tab for your project target.

Step 2: Set Up an Apple Developer Account

To publish apps on the App Store, you need to register as an Apple Developer. Follow these steps:

  1. Go to the Apple Developer website and sign in with your Apple ID.
  2. Enroll in the Apple Developer Program, which costs an annual fee.
  3. Once enrolled, you can create App Store Connect accounts and publish apps.

Step 3: Configure App Icons

Configure the app icon by providing the appropriate sizes for iOS. Icons must be placed in the ios/Runner/Assets.xcassets/AppIcon.appiconset folder. You can generate iOS app icons using tools like appicon.co.

Step 4: Set Up the Launch Screen

iOS apps require a launch screen, which is displayed while the app loads. You can configure this in the LaunchScreen.storyboard located in the ios/Runner folder. You may customize the storyboard to show a static image or branding of your app.

Step 5: Create an App Store Connect Listing

App Store Connect is the interface for managing your app on the App Store. Follow these steps to create a new app listing:

  1. Go to App Store Connect and log in with your developer account.
  2. Click My Apps and then the + (Plus) button to create a new app.
  3. Fill in the app details such as the app name, primary language, bundle ID (configured in Xcode), and SKU.
  4. Select the appropriate pricing and availability options for your app.

Step 6: Configure Signing and Capabilities

Open your project in Xcode (ios/Runner.xcworkspace) and navigate to the Signing & Capabilities tab for your app target. Here you will set up code signing, which is required to distribute the app:

  • Ensure the correct team (your developer account) is selected under the Team dropdown.
  • Ensure that Automatically manage signing is checked. This will create and use a development certificate, a distribution certificate, and a provisioning profile as needed.

Step 7: Build the iOS Release Version

To build a release version of your app, run the following command in your terminal:

flutter build ios --release

This command will create a release build and generate the necessary files for submission to the App Store.

Step 8: Archive the App in Xcode

Once the release build is complete, follow these steps to create an archive of your app:

  1. Open your project in Xcode.
  2. Choose the Runner target and set the Build Configuration to Release.
  3. Select Product > Archive. Xcode will bundle your app into an archive file that is ready for distribution.

Step 9: Upload Your App to App Store Connect

After the archive is created, Xcode will open the Organizer window. Follow these steps to upload your app:

  1. Select your app archive from the list and click Distribute App.
  2. Choose App Store Connect as the method of distribution.
  3. Select Upload to send the app to App Store Connect.

Step 10: Submit for Review

Once your app is uploaded, go back to App Store Connect and complete the following steps:

  1. Fill in all necessary app information such as the description, screenshots, and keywords.
  2. Navigate to the Pricing and Availability section to set the pricing of your app.
  3. Submit your app for review by the Apple team. This process can take several days.

Step 11: Handle App Review Feedback

If your app is approved, it will be available on the App Store. If it's rejected, you'll receive feedback on what needs to be fixed. Address the issues, rebuild your app, and resubmit it for review.

Step 12: Monitor App Analytics

After your app is published, you can monitor its performance through App Store Connect. This includes download statistics, user ratings, and crash reports. Use these insights to improve your app with future updates.

Conclusion

Publishing a Flutter app for iOS requires setting up your Apple Developer account, configuring your app in Xcode, and following Apple's guidelines for app submission. By following this guide, you can successfully release your Flutter app to the iOS App Store.
