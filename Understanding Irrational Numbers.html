Rational & Irrational Numbers

Understanding Irrational Numbers

Irrational numbers are numbers that cannot be expressed as a simple fraction. Unlike rational numbers, which can be written as a ratio of two integers, irrational numbers have decimal expansions that are non-repeating and infinite.

1. Definition

An irrational number is a number that cannot be written as a fraction a/b where a and b are integers, and b is not zero. In other words, irrational numbers cannot be expressed as a simple ratio of two integers.

Characteristics of Irrational Numbers:

  • Non-Terminating: Their decimal representation goes on forever without ending.
  • Non-Repeating: The decimal expansion does not form a repeating pattern.

2. Examples of Irrational Numbers

  • π (Pi): The ratio of the circumference of a circle to its diameter. π is approximately 3.14159, but its decimal expansion is infinite and non-repeating.
  • √2 (Square Root of 2): The number that, when multiplied by itself, equals 2. Its decimal expansion is approximately 1.41421, and it is non-repeating and infinite.
  • √3 (Square Root of 3): The number that, when multiplied by itself, equals 3. Its decimal expansion is approximately 1.73205, and it is also non-repeating and infinite.

3. Properties of Irrational Numbers

  • Non-Repeating Decimals: The decimal expansion does not repeat and does not terminate.
  • Cannot be Expressed as Fractions: They cannot be written as a ratio of two integers.
  • Unique Representations: Each irrational number has a unique and infinite decimal representation.

4. Applications of Irrational Numbers

  • Geometry: Irrational numbers are crucial in geometry, particularly in calculations involving circles, such as determining areas and circumferences using π.
  • Engineering: Irrational numbers are used in engineering calculations where precise measurements are required.
  • Mathematics: They play a role in various mathematical theories and calculations, including calculus and number theory.

Summary

Irrational numbers are numbers that cannot be expressed as a simple fraction and have non-repeating, infinite decimal expansions. They are essential in various fields such as geometry, engineering, and advanced mathematics. Understanding irrational numbers helps in solving a wide range of mathematical problems and appreciating the complexities of numerical systems.
