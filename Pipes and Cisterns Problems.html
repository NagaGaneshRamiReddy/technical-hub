Pipes and Cisterns Problems

Pipes and cisterns problems involve determining how long it takes to fill or empty a tank (cistern) using one or more pipes. These problems often include scenarios where pipes are opened or closed, and you need to calculate the total time for the tank to be filled or emptied under various conditions.

Key Concepts

1. Work Rate of Pipes

The work rate of a pipe is the fraction of the tank it can fill or empty in one hour. If a pipe can fill the tank in T hours, its work rate is:

Work Rate of Pipe:
Work Rate = 1 / T

Where:
T: Total time to fill or empty the tank.

2. Combined Work Rate

When multiple pipes are working together, their combined work rate is the sum of their individual work rates. If Pipe A can fill the tank in T1 hours and Pipe B can fill it in T2 hours, their combined work rate is:

Combined Work Rate:
Combined Work Rate = (1 / T1) + (1 / T2)

3. Filling and Emptying Together

If a pipe is used to fill the tank and another to empty it, you need to subtract the work rate of the emptying pipe from the work rate of the filling pipe:

Net Work Rate:
Net Work Rate = (1 / T1) - (1 / T2)

To find the time required to fill or empty the tank, take the reciprocal of the net work rate:

Time Required:
Time = 1 / Net Work Rate
Example:
Pipe A can fill a tank in 4 hours, and Pipe B can empty it in 6 hours. To find the time required to fill the tank when both pipes are open:
Work Rate of Pipe A = 1 / 4
Work Rate of Pipe B = 1 / 6
Net Work Rate = (1 / 4) - (1 / 6) = 3 / 12 - 2 / 12 = 1 / 12
Time = 1 / (1 / 12) = 12 hours
Therefore, it will take 12 hours to fill the tank with both pipes open.

4. Pipes Working Separately and Together

When pipes work separately and then together, or if one pipe is closed midway, you need to calculate the work done during each phase and then find the remaining work:

Example:
Pipe A can fill a tank in 5 hours, and Pipe B can fill it in 10 hours. If both pipes are opened and Pipe A is closed after 3 hours, how long will it take to fill the tank completely?
Combined Work Rate = (1 / 5) + (1 / 10) = 2 / 10 + 1 / 10 = 3 / 10
Work done in 3 hours = 3 Ã— (3 / 10) = 9 / 10
Remaining work = 1 - 9 / 10 = 1 / 10
Time required by Pipe B to complete the remaining work = (1 / 10) / (1 / 10) = 1 hour
Therefore, it will take 3 hours with both pipes and 1 hour with Pipe B alone to fill the tank.

Applications of Pipes and Cisterns Problems

  • Engineering: Designing efficient water supply systems by calculating the required capacity and flow rates.
  • Construction: Estimating the time needed to fill large tanks or reservoirs during construction.
  • Home Maintenance: Understanding the efficiency of plumbing systems and predicting water filling times.
