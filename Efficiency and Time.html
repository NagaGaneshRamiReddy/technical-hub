Efficiency and Time

Efficiency in the context of work problems refers to how effectively a person or machine performs a task. It is often used to compare the productivity of different workers or machines. Time is the duration required to complete a task, and it is closely related to efficiency.

Key Concepts

1. Definition of Efficiency

Efficiency is a measure of how well a person or machine completes a task within a given time. It can be quantified as the ratio of work done to the time taken. In other words, it indicates how quickly and effectively the work is being completed.

2. Efficiency Formula

The efficiency of a person or machine is calculated using the following formula:

Efficiency Formula:
Efficiency = (1 / Time) × 100%

Where:
Time: Total time taken to complete the task.

Example:
If a machine completes a task in 8 hours, its efficiency is:
Efficiency = (1 / 8) × 100% = 12.5%
This means the machine is working at an efficiency of 12.5% for that task.

3. Relation Between Efficiency and Time

Efficiency and time are inversely related. As the efficiency of a worker or machine increases, the time required to complete the task decreases, and vice versa. The formula for calculating the time based on efficiency is:

Time Formula:
Time = 1 / Efficiency
Example:
If a worker has an efficiency of 20%, then the time required to complete the task is:
Time = 1 / 0.20 = 5 hours
This indicates that the worker will take 5 hours to complete the task at this efficiency level.

4. Comparative Efficiency

To compare the efficiency of two or more workers or machines, you can use the efficiency formula and calculate the time required for each. The worker or machine with the higher efficiency will complete the task in less time.

Example:
Worker A completes a task in 6 hours, and Worker B completes the same task in 4 hours. To compare their efficiencies:
Efficiency of Worker A = (1 / 6) × 100% = 16.67%
Efficiency of Worker B = (1 / 4) × 100% = 25%
Worker B is more efficient than Worker A, as Worker B completes the task faster.

Applications of Efficiency and Time

  • Manufacturing: Determining the productivity of machines and workers to optimize production schedules.
  • Project Management: Assessing the efficiency of team members to ensure timely project completion.
  • Resource Allocation: Allocating resources based on the efficiency of workers or machines to improve overall performance.
