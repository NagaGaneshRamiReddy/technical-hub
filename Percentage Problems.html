Percentage Problems

Percentage problems are commonly encountered in various real-life situations, from shopping discounts to calculating tax and interest rates. Understanding how to solve these problems is essential for managing finances and making informed decisions.

1. Calculating Percentage of a Number

To find a specific percentage of a number, you multiply the number by the percentage and divide by 100.

Formula:
Percentage of a Number = (Number × Percentage) / 100

Example:
If you want to find 15% of ₹800, you calculate:

Percentage of a Number = (₹800 × 15) / 100 = ₹120.
Therefore, 15% of ₹800 is ₹120.

2. Finding the Whole from a Percentage

To find the whole amount when you know a percentage of it, divide the known amount by the percentage and then multiply by 100.

Formula:
Whole Amount = (Part / Percentage) × 100

Example:
If ₹250 is 20% of an amount, to find the whole amount:

Whole Amount = (₹250 / 20) × 100 = ₹1,250.
Therefore, ₹250 is 20% of ₹1,250.

3. Calculating Percentage Increase

To find out how much a value has increased in percentage terms, use the increase amount and divide it by the original value, then multiply by 100.

Formula:
Percentage Increase = [(New Value - Original Value) / Original Value] × 100%

Example:
If the price of a book increased from ₹500 to ₹600:

Increase = ₹600 - ₹500 = ₹100.
Percentage Increase = (₹100 / ₹500) × 100% = 20%.
Therefore, the percentage increase in the price of the book is 20%.

4. Calculating Percentage Decrease

To determine how much a value has decreased in percentage terms, use the decrease amount and divide it by the original value, then multiply by 100.

Formula:
Percentage Decrease = [(Original Value - New Value) / Original Value] × 100%

Example:
If the price of a phone decreased from ₹15,000 to ₹12,000:

Decrease = ₹15,000 - ₹12,000 = ₹3,000.
Percentage Decrease = (₹3,000 / ₹15,000) × 100% = 20%.
Therefore, the percentage decrease in the price of the phone is 20%.

5. Calculating Discounted Price

To find the price after a discount is applied, subtract the discount amount from the marked price.

Formula:
Discounted Price = Marked Price - Discount Amount

Example:
If a shirt is marked at ₹1,200 and a discount of 25% is applied:

Discount Amount = (₹1,200 × 25) / 100 = ₹300.
Discounted Price = ₹1,200 - ₹300 = ₹900.
Therefore, after a 25% discount, the shirt costs ₹900.

Steps to Solve Percentage Problems

  • Identify the Key Values: Determine the original amount, percentage, and any changes or discounts.
  • Choose the Correct Formula: Depending on the problem, use the appropriate formula for percentage calculations.
  • Perform the Calculation: Substitute the values into the formula and solve for the result.
  • Interpret the Result: Make sure the result makes sense in the context of the problem.

Real-Life Applications

Percentage calculations are widely used in everyday life:

  • Shopping: Calculating discounts and understanding sale prices.
  • Finance: Determining interest rates and investment growth.
  • Budgeting: Tracking expenses and savings.
