Work Problems

Work problems involve calculating the amount of time required for individuals or machines to complete a task when working together or separately. The key concept is understanding how work rates combine to determine total time.

Key Concepts

1. Work Rate

The work rate is the fraction of the task that can be completed in a given time. If a person can complete a task in T hours, their work rate is:

Work Rate:
Work Rate = 1 / T

Where:
T: Total time to complete the task.

2. Combined Work Rate

When multiple people or machines work together, their combined work rate is the sum of their individual work rates. If Person A can complete the task in T1 hours and Person B can complete it in T2 hours, their combined work rate is:

Combined Work Rate:
Combined Work Rate = (1 / T1) + (1 / T2)

3. Time to Complete Task

To find the time required for multiple people or machines working together to complete the task, you use the combined work rate formula and take its reciprocal:

Time to Complete Task:
Time = 1 / Combined Work Rate
Example:
If Person A can complete a task in 4 hours and Person B can complete it in 6 hours, their combined work rate is:
Combined Work Rate = (1 / 4) + (1 / 6) = 3 / 12 + 2 / 12 = 5 / 12
Thus, the time to complete the task together is:
Time = 1 / (5 / 12) = 12 / 5 = 2.4 hours
Therefore, working together, they can complete the task in 2.4 hours.

4. Working Separately and Together

When individuals or machines work together and then separately, you can calculate the remaining work after a certain period. For example, if two machines work together for some time and then one machine continues alone, you can find out how much time the remaining work will take.

Example:
If Machine A and Machine B work together and complete 3/4 of the task in 2 hours, and then Machine A continues alone, you need to find out how long Machine A will take to finish the remaining 1/4 of the task.
Let’s assume Machine A takes 6 hours to complete the task alone. So, its work rate is 1/6 per hour.
The remaining work is 1/4. Time required for Machine A to complete this remaining work is:
Time = (1 / 4) / (1 / 6) = 6 / 4 = 1.5 hours
Therefore, Machine A will take 1.5 hours to finish the remaining work alone.

Applications of Work Problems

  • Project Management: Estimating the time required to complete projects with multiple contributors.
  • Engineering: Calculating the time for machines to complete tasks in manufacturing processes.
  • Team Work: Determining how long a team will need to finish a given task if members work at different rates.
