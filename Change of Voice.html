Change of Voice

Understanding Change of Voice

Change of Voice involves transforming sentences between the active and passive voice. This is an important aspect of English grammar that helps in enhancing writing clarity and style.

Key Concepts

1. Active Voice

In the active voice, the subject performs the action of the verb. For example, "The cat (subject) chased (verb) the mouse (object)." The focus is on the subject performing the action.

2. Passive Voice

In the passive voice, the subject receives the action of the verb. For example, "The mouse (subject) was chased (verb) by the cat (agent)." The focus shifts to the object receiving the action.

3. Rules for Changing Voice

  • Identify the subject, verb, and object in the sentence.
  • Move the object to the subject position and the subject to the agent position.
  • Change the verb form to the appropriate passive form (e.g., "is chased" instead of "chases").
  • Include the word "by" before the original subject in the passive voice, if necessary.

Examples

Example 1:

Active Voice: "The chef (subject) cooks (verb) the meal (object)."
Passive Voice: "The meal (subject) is cooked (verb) by the chef (agent)."

Example 2:

Active Voice: "The teacher (subject) explains (verb) the lesson (object)."
Passive Voice: "The lesson (subject) is explained (verb) by the teacher (agent)."

Example 3:

Active Voice: "The company (subject) will announce (verb) the results (object)."
Passive Voice: "The results (subject) will be announced (verb) by the company (agent)."

Common Pitfalls

1. Incorrect Verb Form

Incorrect verb form can occur if the passive construction is not properly applied. Ensure that the verb tense in the passive voice matches the tense of the active voice.

2. Omission of the Agent

Omitting the agent (the doer of the action) can sometimes lead to ambiguity. If it's important to specify the agent, include it using the word "by."

3. Overuse of Passive Voice

Overusing passive voice can make writing less engaging. Use passive voice judiciously and consider the impact on readability.
