Google Cloud Identity and Access Management

Introduction to Google Cloud IAM

Google Cloud Identity and Access Management (IAM) provides a unified view into security policy across your cloud resources, with built-in auditing to ensure compliance. IAM allows you to manage who has access to your Google Cloud resources, what actions they can perform, and under what conditions.

Key Concepts

  • Identity: Represents a user, group, or service account that interacts with your resources. Identities can be internal (Google users and groups) or external (Google Accounts, service accounts, or third-party identities).
  • Role: Defines a set of permissions that can be granted to an identity. Roles can be predefined (by Google) or custom (created by you to meet specific needs).
  • Policy: A policy is a collection of roles assigned to identities. IAM policies define what actions are allowed or denied for a particular identity on a specific resource.
  • Permission: An action that a role grants. Permissions are associated with roles and specify what operations can be performed on resources.
  • Service Account: A special kind of account used by applications and virtual machines to interact with Google Cloud services. Service accounts are used for automated interactions with cloud resources.

How IAM Works

IAM operates by assigning roles to identities at the resource level. When an identity attempts to perform an action, IAM checks the policies attached to the resource to determine if the identity has the required permissions. If the permissions are granted, the action is allowed; otherwise, it is denied.

Common IAM Roles

  • Owner: Has full access to all resources and permissions, including the ability to manage roles and permissions.
  • Editor: Can modify resources but cannot manage roles and permissions.
  • Viewer: Can view resources but cannot make changes.
  • Custom Roles: Created to tailor permissions specific to your needs, providing a finer level of access control.

Managing IAM

  • Granting Roles: Assign roles to identities using the Google Cloud Console, gcloud command-line tool, or the IAM API. Assign roles at different levels (organization, folder, project, resource) as needed.
  • Reviewing Access: Regularly review and audit IAM policies to ensure they meet your security and compliance requirements. Use tools like Cloud Audit Logs to monitor access and changes.
  • Best Practices: Follow the principle of least privilege by granting only the minimum permissions necessary for a role. Regularly update roles and policies based on changing needs.

Summary

Google Cloud IAM is a powerful tool for managing access to your cloud resources. By understanding key concepts such as identities, roles, policies, and permissions, you can effectively control who has access to what resources and what actions they can perform. Regularly reviewing and managing IAM policies ensures that your cloud environment remains secure and compliant.
