Understanding Assertion and Reason

Assertion and Reason is a type of logical reasoning question where you are given two statements: an assertion and a reason. Your task is to determine the relationship between these two statements based on a set of given options.

Key Concepts in Assertion and Reason

1. Assertion

An assertion is a statement that declares a fact or opinion. It is a statement that can be evaluated as true or false.

2. Reason

A reason is a statement that provides an explanation or justification for the assertion. It supports or refutes the assertion and can also be evaluated as true or false.

Types of Relationships

In assertion and reason questions, you need to understand the following relationships:

  • Assertion is True, and Reason is True; Reason is the Correct Explanation for Assertion: Both statements are true, and the reason properly explains why the assertion is true.
  • Assertion is True, and Reason is True; Reason is Not the Correct Explanation for Assertion: Both statements are true, but the reason does not adequately explain the assertion.
  • Assertion is True, and Reason is False: The assertion is true, but the reason is incorrect or not true.
  • Assertion is False, and Reason is True: The assertion is incorrect, but the reason is true.
  • Assertion is False, and Reason is False: Both the assertion and the reason are incorrect.

Steps to Solve Assertion and Reason Problems

1. Analyze the Assertion

Evaluate the truthfulness of the assertion. Determine if it is a true or false statement based on the information provided.

2. Evaluate the Reason

Determine if the reason provided is true or false. Check if the reason logically supports or contradicts the assertion.

3. Determine the Relationship

Based on the truthfulness of both statements, identify the correct relationship between the assertion and the reason using the given options.

Practice Example

Example:

Assertion: All birds can fly.
Reason: Birds have wings.

Options:

  • A. Assertion is True, and Reason is True; Reason is the Correct Explanation for Assertion.
  • B. Assertion is True, and Reason is True; Reason is Not the Correct Explanation for Assertion.
  • C. Assertion is True, and Reason is False.
  • D. Assertion is False, and Reason is True.
  • E. Assertion is False, and Reason is False.

Solution: The assertion is False because not all birds can fly (e.g., ostriches). The reason is True, but it does not justify the assertion. Therefore, the correct option is E.
