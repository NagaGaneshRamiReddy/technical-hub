Pipes and Cisterns

Introduction to Pipes and Cisterns

Pipes and cisterns are concepts used to solve problems related to filling and emptying tanks or containers. These problems typically involve calculating the time required to fill or empty a tank given the rate at which water flows through pipes or drains. Understanding these concepts is essential for efficient management of water resources and infrastructure.

Basic Concepts

In pipes and cisterns problems, you often need to determine how long it will take to fill or empty a cistern given the rates of multiple pipes (inlets and outlets) working together or separately.

Formulas

1. Filling a Cistern

To calculate the time required to fill a cistern with one or more pipes:

Formula to Calculate Time to Fill a Cistern:
Time = Volume of Cistern / (Rate of Inlet Pipe)

Volume of Cistern: The total capacity of the cistern.
Rate of Inlet Pipe: The rate at which the pipe fills the cistern (e.g., liters per hour).

2. Emptying a Cistern

To calculate the time required to empty a cistern with one or more pipes:

Formula to Calculate Time to Empty a Cistern:
Time = Volume of Cistern / (Rate of Outlet Pipe)

Volume of Cistern: The total capacity of the cistern.
Rate of Outlet Pipe: The rate at which the pipe empties the cistern (e.g., liters per hour).

3. Mixed Problems

When multiple pipes are involved, both filling and emptying simultaneously, the effective rate is calculated as:

Effective Rate = Rate of Inlet Pipe - Rate of Outlet Pipe
Time to Fill/Empty = Volume of Cistern / Effective Rate

Rate of Inlet Pipe: The rate at which the cistern is filled.
Rate of Outlet Pipe: The rate at which the cistern is emptied.

Examples

Example 1:
If a cistern has a capacity of 1000 liters and a single pipe fills it at a rate of 200 liters per hour, the time required to fill the cistern is:
Time = 1000 liters / 200 liters per hour = 5 hours
Therefore, it will take 5 hours to fill the cistern.
Example 2:
If a cistern with a capacity of 1200 liters has a pipe that can empty it at a rate of 300 liters per hour, the time required to empty the cistern is:
Time = 1200 liters / 300 liters per hour = 4 hours
Therefore, it will take 4 hours to empty the cistern.
Example 3:
A cistern has a capacity of 1500 liters. Pipe A can fill it in 6 hours, and Pipe B can empty it in 10 hours. If both pipes are open together, the time to fill the cistern is:
Effective Rate = (1/6 - 1/10) cisterns per hour = (5 - 3) / 30 = 2 / 30 = 1 / 15 cisterns per hour
Time = 1 / Effective Rate = 15 hours
Therefore, it will take 15 hours to fill the cistern with both pipes open.

How to Solve Pipes and Cisterns Problems

  • Determine the Capacity: Find out the total capacity of the cistern.
  • Identify Rates: Determine the filling and emptying rates of the pipes.
  • Apply Formulas: Use the formulas for filling, emptying, or mixed problems based on the given rates.
  • Calculate Time: Compute the time required using the formulas and effective rates.

Why are Pipes and Cisterns Important?

  • Water Management: Helps in planning and managing water resources efficiently.
  • Infrastructure Planning: Useful for designing and optimizing systems for water storage and distribution.
  • Efficiency Improvement: Ensures effective use of resources and reduces wastage in water-related tasks.

Real-Life Example

Consider a water tank at a residential building:

Scenario:
- A water tank needs to be filled by 3 different pipes with different flow rates.
- Pipe A fills at 100 liters per hour, Pipe B at 150 liters per hour, and Pipe C at 200 liters per hour.
- If Pipe A is open for 4 hours, Pipe B for 3 hours, and Pipe C for 2 hours, how much water is added to the tank?

Water Added = (100 liters/hour × 4 hours) + (150 liters/hour × 3 hours) + (200 liters/hour × 2 hours)
Water Added = 400 liters + 450 liters + 400 liters = 1250 liters

Therefore, a total of 1250 liters of water is added to the tank.
