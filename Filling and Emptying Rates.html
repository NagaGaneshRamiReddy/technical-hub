Filling and Emptying Rates

Filling and emptying rates refer to the speed at which a tank or cistern is filled or emptied by one or more pipes. These problems typically involve calculating how long it takes to complete the filling or emptying process under various conditions.

Key Concepts

1. Rate of Filling

The rate of filling describes how quickly a pipe can fill a tank. If a pipe can fill a tank in T hours, its rate of filling is:

Rate of Filling:
Rate of Filling = 1 / T

Where:
T: Total time required to fill the tank.

Example:
If a pipe can fill a tank in 5 hours, its rate of filling is:
Rate of Filling = 1 / 5 = 0.20
This means the pipe fills 20% of the tank per hour.

2. Rate of Emptying

The rate of emptying describes how quickly a pipe can empty a tank. If a pipe can empty a tank in T hours, its rate of emptying is:

Rate of Emptying:
Rate of Emptying = 1 / T

Where:
T: Total time required to empty the tank.

Example:
If a pipe can empty a tank in 4 hours, its rate of emptying is:
Rate of Emptying = 1 / 4 = 0.25
This means the pipe empties 25% of the tank per hour.

3. Combined Rate of Filling and Emptying

When multiple pipes are involved, their rates of filling and emptying are combined. If multiple filling pipes and/or emptying pipes are working together, their rates are added or subtracted accordingly:

Combined Rate:
Combined Rate = (Sum of Filling Rates) - (Sum of Emptying Rates)
Example:
Pipe A fills a tank in 6 hours, and Pipe B empties it in 8 hours. The combined rate when both pipes are open is:
Rate of Filling by Pipe A = 1 / 6
Rate of Emptying by Pipe B = 1 / 8
Combined Rate = (1 / 6) - (1 / 8) = 4 / 24 - 3 / 24 = 1 / 24
This means that together, they fill 1/24 of the tank per hour.

4. Calculating Time Based on Combined Rates

To find the time required to fill or empty the tank based on the combined rate, use the formula:

Time Required:
Time = 1 / Combined Rate
Example:
Using the combined rate from the previous example (1 / 24), the time required to fill the tank is:
Time = 1 / (1 / 24) = 24 hours
Therefore, it will take 24 hours to fill the tank with both pipes working together.

Applications of Filling and Emptying Rates

  • Plumbing: Designing and optimizing water supply systems.
  • Construction: Estimating the time required for filling or emptying large containers.
  • Event Planning: Calculating the time needed to prepare large tanks or pools.
